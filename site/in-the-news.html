We are saddened to announce that a member of the LaSalle Bakery family passed away in February after a battling a short illness. A beloved co-worker and friend, Paula (Coyle) Decurtis began working at the bakery as part of the sales team at the age of 17. She was one of Michael and Cheryl’s very first hires, and she soon became an important part of their lives. Paula was an excellent worker – she was loyal, trustworthy, and she loved working at the bakery. She possessed many skills, including her affinity for cake decorating. She was trained in-house as a cake decorator, and quickly became the top cake decorator in her field. Paula earned numerous awards at industry competitions, and we’re sure she made at least one of your special occasion cakes. For the 37 years that she worked with us, Paula’s passion and enthusiasm for cake decorating and for the bakery never wavered – she loved what she did.Our hearts are heavy with her passing. Paula was a good friend, co-worker, mother, and wife. She will be missed very much by everyone.

LaSalle Bakery Has the Bright Idea to Go Green!

LaSalle Bakery is going green by taking steps to reduce their energy usage by updating their lighting and transitioning to a power-saving HVAC system.

New technology in lighting uses about 75% less energy than a traditional incandescent bulb, this is exactly why owners Michael and Cheryl Manni have decided to make the change and reduce their carbon foot-print.Those that know LaSalle Bakery understand that a significant part of their culture is being an active member in the community. When asked about LaSalle’s green initiatives a bakery customer made a comment that, “I think it’s great that LaSalle takes pride in the community and environment and hope other businesses see this and follow suit.” This is just one of the ways that LaSalle Bakery plays in keeping the Providence community and customers at top of the list with everything they do.
